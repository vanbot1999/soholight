Background

I was inspired by the children of Newburgh, Scotland as they have been designing the Christmas lighting for their high street for over 20 years now - with the agreement of Mrs Shona Gray (Head of Newburgh Action Group), I set up and managed the Soho Kids Xmas Lights project in Soho, London since 2021 in collaboration with the local primary school pupils, which is now in its 3rd annual edition.

The idea for the Soho Kids Xmas Lights project started just before Christmas 2020 from an article I read on BBC News about a town in Newburgh, Scotland where the local primary school pupils have been designing Christmas lights to decorate the high street for over 20 years now. I contacted Mrs Shona Gray (Head of Newburgh Action Group) to find out more, and to ask permission to bring the project to Soho, London in 2021, to Soho Parish Primary School (Artsmark Platinum award 2020) and its pupils. In 2021 and in 2022 the project was very well received by the school and the community. It was a great success showcasing 21 (2021 edition) plus another 14 + 2 commissions (2022 edition) Christmas lighting panels on the streets of Soho designed by the selected 35 pupils from the local primary school. The selection of the pupilsâ€™ entries is made yearly by a jury committee composed of various individuals - local residents, business owners, and Councillors, members of the Parents Teachers Association including Alumni pupils, The National Gallery, The Royal Institute of British Architects (RIBA Learning), designers, myself, and the renowned Daniel Lismore. The Royal Institute of British Architects (RIBA Learning) every year distinguishes some of the pupils lighting designs with an award.
